Myanmar Coffee Table


I built a table that has a map of Myanmar on it. With most of my family living in Myanmar, this table represents that my family will always be there with me. The table is a representation of family living across the globe but still being connected. I faced many challenges throughout the process of making the table (such as properly cutting the wood, or using the bandsaw to cut the exact map pieces out); however, with every new challenge, a new spark of motivation appeared. I take special pride in this table because I felt like I made something with meaning to it, something that will connect me with my family from miles and miles away.

GPS LED Watch


I made this GPS watch during my spare time. So I followed Adafruit’s tutorial on the FLORA GPS watch and made a few of my own modifications. I created the watch to also have a mode of a flashlight and changed some of the colors around. Though there are definitely better watches and GPS devices, this project was a fun way to experiment with the GPS module, neopixel ring, the FLORA board, and an accelerometer.

Solar Powered USB Charger


The project was to design and create a Solar powered USB Charger. Our teacher gave us guidelines to make the actual electronic part, then we would design our own enclosure. Every year, a group of students from a few schools goes to Senegal, Africa. I donated my solar powered USB charger, so that the families in Senegal could use it for whatever they need.

iPhone 6 Minimalist Microscope Attachment


I made this iphone 6 microscope attachment during a summer camp. I designed this keeping functionality, versatility, and simplicity in mind. The front camera microscope is a biconvex lens attachment and the back camera has both a ball lenses and the same biconvex lens as the front. For the back camera, I added a function where you can switch between two different lenses using a simple spinning device. This gives the user a chance to easily choose which lens best suits their needs. This prototype is cheap, portable, and easy to use. It can be used more efficiently for medical and scientific applications with higher quality lenses.

Wireless Muscle Sensor Bike Signal Prototype


In the class called Circuits and Electronics: Device Invention, the assignment was to invent something useful that had not already been invented. Initially, I was going to create a device similar to a back brace for scoliosis, however, the project needed to be scaled back. As I was brainstorming, I recalled the time a few years ago when I fell off my bicycle because I had to lift my arm to signal a turn. I was inspired to create technology that would help amateur bikers have an easier and safer time on the road.
